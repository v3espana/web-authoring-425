Introduction

Take a quick look into my climbing routine, including what I bring with me and what I've been able to accomplish on the wall! It's split into two sections: Chalk Bag Essentials and Recent Routes.

First, I'll focus on some of the small things that make a big difference during a session. Shoes, chalk, and other basics might not seem that important at first, but they can have a big impact on how comfortable and consistent you feel while climbing. This part of the page is meant to give a realistic look at what climbers actually carry, especially for beginners who might not know what they need yet. By no means are these requirements for everyone, but these are definitely what helps me make it through my climbing sessions.

Next is all about progress. These are all about the climbs I'm doing throughout a session, whether that's a flash or a project I spend multiple weeks on. They'll all have different challenges, whether it's balance, strength, or just figuring out a tricky sequence. It's not just about showing off sends, but more about tracking improvement and seeing how far I've come!

Now, let's stop the yapping and get into it!

My softer pair for easier climbs.

To protect my Achilles

EVMT Chalk

My basically empty bag of chalk.

Chalk Ball

Goes inside of the chalk bag.

Metolius Chalk

Backup if I run out.

Grip Strengthener

To warm up the fingers.

Knee Brace

To protect my breaking knees.

Mini First Aid Kit

To patch and scratches or cuts.

These are all of the things that I keep in my chalk bag and use throughout my rock climbing sessions. Some, like my shoes and chalk, I use every time I climb, while I carry around others just for my safety, on and off the wall. By no means do you need all of this stuff, but this is just what I like to have on me when climbing!

Route Status Difficulty Enjoyment Description
Pink V2 Flashed 1/5 3.5/5 This was a softer V2 that I hit at the beginning of one of my sessions. There was a two-hand start on the gray volume that required you to position your body to the left, and then square out for the rest of the climb. The only thing about these holds is that sometimes they're a bit difficult to get your hand around, especially on the larger ones, requiring more friction on the fingers.
Yellow V3 Topped on 3rd Attempt 3/5 4/5 There were not many footholds for this one, so I had a bit of a hard time starting it on the incline. But once you make it past that part, the rest of it isnt too bad. I don't usually like slopers, but these had a big give to them which made them easier to grip.
Yellow V3 DNF 4/5 2/5 I initially avoided this climb because I am not a big fan of pinchers, but for the sake of this website, I wanted to try it anyways. All it did was confirm that I don't have enough grip strength to enjoy pincher climbs. I always have a harder time positioning and trusting my feet on these.
